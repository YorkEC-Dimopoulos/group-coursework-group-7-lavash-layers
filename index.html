The Pink Route

Welcome to Yerevan — a city of pink tuff stone, open-air cafés, and evenings that last long after sunset. It's easy to explore, full of culture, and packed with spots that feel local even on your first week here.

This guide is made for students and first-time visitors who want practical, budget-friendly recommendations: what to do, where to eat, how to get around, and which neighbourhoods match the vibe you’re looking for.

Quick highlights

  • Best first walk: Republic Square → Northern Avenue → Opera area, then finish at the Cascade for views.
  • Sunset plan: grab a takeaway coffee, climb the Cascade terraces, and stay for the city lights.
  • Quick local bite: try a fresh pastry from a bakery, then follow it with a casual shawarma stop.
  • Culture in 2 hours: one museum/gallery in the centre, then a slow evening stroll through the nearby parks.
  • Budget hangout: pick a central park bench, people-watch, and spend your money on dessert instead.
  • Weekend vibe: browse Vernissage Market for crafts and souvenirs, then walk back into the centre for lunch.
  • Want “real city” energy? choose one neighbourhood and explore side streets, courtyards, and small cafés.
  • Short on time: stay in the centre—most key sights are close enough to do on foot in one afternoon.

About this project

This website is a group project created as a student-friendly guide to Yerevan. Our goal is to share practical tips and curated places that help newcomers explore confidently. You can learn more about the team and our work on the About Us page.
