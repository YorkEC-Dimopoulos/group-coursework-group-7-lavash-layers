History & Culture of Armenian Cuisine

Armenian cuisine includes the foods and cooking techniques of the Armenian people and traditional Armenian foods and drinks. The cuisine reflects the history and geography where Armenians have lived and where Armenian empires existed. The cuisine also reflects the traditional crops and animals grown and raised in Armenian-populated or controlled areas.

Origins of Armenian Cuisine

Armenian food has roots in ancient traditions, influenced by geography, climate, and neigboring cultures. Many recipes have been passed down through generations.

Food for Travelers

Many Armenian dishes, like Khash and Dolma, were originally designed for travelers. Ingredients and cooking methods ensured the food could stay fresh for long journeys.

Armenian Wine

The alcoholic drink with the longest history in Armenia is wine. The oldest known winery in the world was discovered in Armenia. Historically, wineries in Armenia were concentrated along the Ararat valley. Of particular note was the district of Koghtn (Գողթն, current Nakhichevan area). Today, Armenian wineries are concentrated in the Areni region (district of Vayots Dzor).

Mineral Waters

Armenia has rich reserves of mineral water. After the establishment of the Soviet Union the study and development of multilateral disciplines in these waters began.
