WHAT TO DO IN YEREVAN?

Yerevan is a city that’s easy to explore on foot, especially in the centre where many of its best-known sights are close to each other. From Republic Square and its museums to the Cascade’s panoramic viewpoints, the city offers a mix of history, art, and lively public spaces that reveal its character. Cultural landmarks, walkable streets, open-air markets, and local favourites all come together to create an experience that feels both historic and modern—perfect for visitors who want to see the city’s everyday atmosphere as well as its most iconic places.

The city-centre cultural

Republic Square

Republic Square is the perfect starting point in central Yerevan, and it’s also home to two top museums: the History Museum of Armenia for a clear look at Armenia’s past, and the National Gallery of Armenia for Armenian and international art. Together, they offer an easy, walkable introduction to the city’s culture.

Northern Avenue

Northern Avenue is a modern pedestrian street that connects Republic Square with the Opera area, making it an easy and pleasant walk through central Yerevan. Lined with cafés, shops, and open spaces, it’s a great place to take a break, people-watch, and enjoy the city’s everyday atmosphere while moving between major attractions.

Opera and Freedom Square

The Opera and Freedom Square area is one of Yerevan’s main cultural hubs, centered around the Armenian National Opera and Ballet Theatre. The surrounding open space and nearby park are perfect for a relaxed walk, photos, and a short break before continuing toward the Cascade and other central attractions.

Cascade Complex

The Cascade Complex is one of Yerevan’s most famous landmarks, combining giant terraces, outdoor sculptures, and some of the best views over the city. Inside, the Cafesjian Center for the Arts adds galleries and exhibitions, so the Cascade feels like both a viewpoint and an open-air museum in the heart of Yerevan.

Matenadaran

Matenadaran (the Mesrop Mashtots Institute of Ancient Manuscripts) is one of Yerevan’s most important cultural sites, dedicated to preserving and displaying Armenia’s written heritage. Its museum exhibits beautifully decorated manuscripts and historical documents, making it a great visit if you want a deeper understanding of Armenian language, history, and scholarship.
