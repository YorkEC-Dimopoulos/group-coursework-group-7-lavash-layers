Armenian Cuisine

Armenian cuisine reflects the country's long history, rich culture, and use of fresh ingredients. In Yerevan, meals are not just food - they are a social experience, shared with family and friends. Traditional recipes have been passed down through generations.

Dishes Restaurants Food History Street Food & Cafes

Khorovats

Khorovats is the Armenian version of barbecue, made with marinated meat grilled over an open flame. It is a must-try dish during celebrations and family gatherings.

Dolma

Dolma consists of grape leaves or vegetables stuffed with minced meat, rice, and herbs. It is one of the most iconic Armenian dishes.

Khash

Khash is a traditional Armenian soup made from boiled cow or sheep feet, often enjoyed in the early morning during winter months. It is a symbol of hospitality and is usually served with garlic, salt, and lavash bread. Khash gatherings are social events, bringing family and friends together to share warmth and tradition.

Kyufta

Armenian-style meatballs, usually fried or baked. Served in small eateries or street stalls.

Harissa

Shawarma is a popular street food in Yerevan, made with marinated meat (usually chicken or beef) cooked on a vertical rotisserie, then wrapped in lavash with fresh vegetables and sauces. Perfect for a quick, flavorful meal on the go.

Ghapama

Ghapama is a festive Armenian dish made from pumpkin with rice, dried fruits, nuts, and honey. It is traditionally prepared during celebrations and symbolizes abundance and hospitality in Armenian culture.
