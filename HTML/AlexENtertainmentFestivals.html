Festivals and holidays to experience in Yerevan

If you visit at the right time of year, Yerevan’s centre turns into a big open-air celebration—perfect for adding some “living culture” to your sightseeing route. Here are three of the most popular events to mention on your page.

Yerevan Wine Days (June)

Yerevan Wine Days is Armenia’s largest open-air wine festival, held in the first weekend of June right in the city centre. For three days, streets like Saryan, Tumanyan, and Moskovyan become a walkable festival zone with wine tastings, food, music, and a lively crowd—great even if you just want to walk around and feel the atmosphere.

Vardavar (summer water festival)

Vardavar is Armenia’s famous summer tradition where people splash each other with water—it’s playful, family-friendly, and happens 14 weeks (98 days) after Easter, usually in July. In Yerevan, the biggest “water battles” often form around places with easy water access, including central squares and fountains, so you can experience it even while staying in the city centre.

Erebuni–Yerevan (autumn)

Every autumn, Yerevan celebrates its “birthday” with the Erebuni–Yerevan festivities, marking the city’s founding in 782 BC. The celebration typically includes concerts, performances, and city-centre events that bring a festival mood to streets and squares—an ideal time to explore central Yerevan with extra energy and public celebrations happening around you.

Music Fest with Coca-Cola

Music Fest with Coca-Cola is one of Yerevan’s biggest ticketed open-air music events, bringing together popular Armenian and international artists for hours of live performances. The festival is designed as an all-in-one experience with a large concert setup plus extra zones like food & drinks areas and a dedicated kids’ zone, so it works both as a music night and a full festival atmosphere. Dates and venues can vary by year, so it’s best to check the current announcement before planning.
