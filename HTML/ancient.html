Ancient Yerevan: Erebuni Fortress and Early History

Yerevan’s commonly cited founding date is 782 BC. This date is linked to the fortress of Erebuni, founded by King Argishti I during the Kingdom of Urartu. Inscriptions and archaeological evidence connect the modern city to this early period.

Erebuni functioned as a fortified administrative center. Excavations indicate planned architecture, storage spaces, and religious areas, suggesting it was part of a wider system of governance and control over nearby land and routes.

Although Urartu later declined, the physical remains of Erebuni remain a strong historical anchor for understanding the earliest documented layer of Yerevan’s past.

Quick facts

  • Date: 782 BC
  • Site: Erebuni Fortress
  • Context: Kingdom of Urartu
  • Evidence: Inscribed records and archaeological findings
