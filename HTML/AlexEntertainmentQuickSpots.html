Quick extra stops near the centre

Vernissage Market

Vernissage Market is Yerevan’s well-known open-air market where you can browse handmade crafts, small artworks, jewelry, and souvenirs. It’s a great stop for getting something unique and local, even if you only have 20–30 minutes to walk around.

Ponchikanots

Ponchikanots is a classic Yerevan stop for a quick, sweet break — famous for ponchik (soft donuts, often filled) and the traditional pairing of hot cocoa. It’s popular with both locals and visitors because it’s simple, affordable, and feels like a small piece of the city’s everyday culture rather than a “tourist-only” place.

Kond (historic district)

Kond is one of Yerevan’s oldest neighborhoods, known for its narrow streets, uneven stairways, and “old city” atmosphere. It’s a great quick stop if you want to see a more authentic side of Yerevan that feels different from the modern centre.

GUM Market (Gumi Shuka)

GUM Market is a lively indoor market where you can see (and taste) everyday Yerevan—fresh fruit, dried goods, spices, and local snacks. It’s perfect for a short visit when you want local vibes, quick souvenirs, or something to eat on the go.

Swan Lake (near the Opera)

Swan Lake is a small, relaxing spot next to the Opera area, popular for sitting, photos, and a calm break during a city walk. It’s especially nice in the evening when the area feels lively but not rushed.

Charles Aznavour Square (Moscow Cinema area)

Aznavour Square is a central meeting spot with a fun city atmosphere and an easy “walk-by” location during your route. It works well as a quick stop for photos and a short pause before continuing to nearby streets, cafés, or the Opera area.
